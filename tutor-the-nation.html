Tutor the Nation

Tutor the Nation is an organisation that aims to bridge the gap in education by providing free tutorage to under-privileged students by pairing them with volunteers from universities across the UK.

I worked directly with the clients to build their brand, collaborated with our developers to build the site and User feedback was that the site was engaging and memorable.

Logo

“Bridging the gap” in education was a core concept for the clients, so it was a clear starting point for the logo. After experimenting with graphics, played with the word mark to form the H into a dual-purpose motif — that is a bridge and a smile. I probably spent most of the design time for this logo trying to find the perfect curve and alignment that meant it was still readable, but also resembled a bridge.

From there the slogan, “Bridging the gap in education with a smile” came to mind, communicating the positivity of the founders as well as their kind and approachable nature.

Secondary Marks - “Bridget”

“With a smile” was such an important sentiment to the brand that I created a secondary mark for the brand: “Bridget”. She is constructed using the H/smile and cutting the O in half to make her eyes. She comes in two forms, colour block and logo-outline.

The clients wanted the brand to be relatable to their (younger university student audience) and to feel very human. Everyone loved Bridget, and she really helped represent those values.

Primary Colour Palette

Bright, bold and (almost) primary colours create the base of the palette, contrasted by full sections of black and white - the colour palette was aimed around 3 core concepts: exciting, youthful and engaging.

Stickers!

I found that the blocky design of the website could be broken up with hand-drawn stickers, each symbolising features of the company, from safeguarding to confidence (and of course sticker-Bridget). They can also be reused as fun recognisable brand assets. We had great feedback from the target audience about them!

Social Media Templates

During the scoping phase we found that Social media would be an important part of Tutor the Nation reaching their target: to recruit new volunteers.

Using bold colour block text mixed in with black and white imagery (and stickers) these templates are designed to be fun and informative to catch the eye of potential tutors and schools.

The Website

The site design utilises colour blocking, full bleed sections, photography and big bold text to create an open and human centred design with the main goal of recruiting new volunteers/tutors. Information is clearly labeled with easy to find FAQs and contact information.

Accessibility is important, so to create contrast a secondary colour palette was introduced using HSL and font sizes were kept at a larger size. The buttons were rounded and the uplifted/curved titles soften the sharper shapes in the site making it feel balanced and comfortable.

The uplifted titles are meant to symbolise upward momentum, climbing up and “over lap the gap”. It might not be an obvious sentiment to users but

the inference is there and keeps users engaged as they scroll through the site, giving importance to new sections.

Mobile

On mobile the blocks become stacks, this helped to keep the design consistent on all devices and just as user-friendly and open.

As the site doubles as a hub for current tutors to log into their accounts the log in page was mobile optimised.

Wireframes

The wireframes for this site were a bit more UI than normal - the client requested to see something that was a bit more hi-res and included the stylised “uplifted” titles, typography, photography and logo designs.

The clients were extremely happy with the wireframes and said that they could really get the feeling for the final product, it was a relief the final site exceeded their expectations based off the wireframes.
